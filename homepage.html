Flowers Or Blossoms?

Everyone loves flowers. They look good, 
they smell good, and if you pick the right flowers 
(pineapple sage, anyone?) they even taste good. 
Flowers play symbolic roles in all sorts of human rituals: 
rose bouquets at weddings, white lilies at funerals, 
bright orange marigolds at Mexican or Hindu festivals.
Flowers are so symbolised in our culture that 
we often forget the ecological, biological roles flowers play.
Flowers are very important to the plants that bear them.
For most plants, flowers attract pollinators to make seeds,
helping plants to reproduce and form the next generation. 
Flowers also play important roles in ecosystems. 
Floral nectar, pollen and even petals are 
an important food source for a huge range of animals, 
from bees and beetles to birds and bats. 
In Australia nectar from our main tree genus, Eucalyptus, 
is a hugely important food source. 
Migratory birds like the Swift Parrot (Lathamus discolor) and 
bats like the Little Red Flying Fox (Pteropus scapulatus) 
often move with eucalypt flowering across the landscape.
